Most small companies are quietly bleeding money on cross-border payments, and most banks have no incentive to tell them.

Hidden FX margins
Your bank quotes you a 'free' wire but takes 2% to 4% inside the rate. On a €100k transfer that is up to €4,000, every time.
Nobody picks up when the rate moves
Banks route small clients to a hotline, not a specialist. By the time someone calls back, the window is gone.
SWIFT fees you only see on the receiving end
Correspondent bank charges, lifting fees, beneficiary deductions. The actual cost of a payment is rarely the one you signed off on.

Your bank quotes a rate, then settles at a different one. The spread sits hidden inside the rate. There is no transparent execution moment, no quote you can hold.

Need to pay a supplier in Vietnam, Brazil, or Pakistan? Most banks either refuse the corridor or charge punishing spreads on emerging-market currencies.

FAQ

Questions small businesses ask us first.

Is there a minimum FX volume to open an account?
No fixed minimum. HansePay works with companies trading anywhere from a few thousand euros a year to several hundred million. The only thing that changes is which products make sense for your flow. We are honest about that during onboarding.
How is HansePay regulated?
HansePay operates under European MiCAR License, supervised in Germany by BaFin. Full licensing details on our Licenses page.
How do you make money if your spreads are so tight?
We charge a small, transparent markup on top of the interbank rate, disclosed before every trade. The savings against typical bank pricing are structural, not promotional. We make less per trade than a house bank but serve customers at any volume.
Can HansePay integrate with my accounting software?
API access is available for institutional customers. Standard format exports work with most accounting workflows. Talk to us about your specific integration needs.
Do you support payments to high-risk countries?
HansePay covers 45+ markets including many emerging markets where standard banks block or surcharge. Every transaction is screened under EU AML and KYC rules. Talk to our specialist about your specific corridor.
What happens to my funds if HansePay fails?
Customer funds are held in segregated accounts at tier-1 European banks, ring-fenced from HansePay's operating capital. In an insolvency, those funds are not part of the insolvency estate. They are legally protected under MiCAR Article 75 and returned at par.
Can I lock a rate at execution?
Yes. The exchange rate is locked at the moment of trade execution. The rate shown in the quote is the rate that executes. No slippage between quote and settlement, and no future-dated locking.

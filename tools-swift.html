Anatomy of a SWIFT code.

A SWIFT/BIC code is either 8 or 11 characters and identifies a specific bank (and optionally a specific branch) anywhere in the world. The first four characters are the bank code — a unique short identifier for the institution. The next two are the ISO country code. Two more identify the city or location of the head office. The optional final three identify a specific branch; if omitted, the code refers to the primary office.

The hidden cost

Where manufacturers lose
margin to FX.

Wide spreads on big trades
Banks typically charge 1.5% to 3% above mid-market on major currency pairs. Multiplied across the year, that's the biggest invisible expense on every shipment.
No transparent execution moment
Banks quote a rate, then settle at a different one. The spread sits hidden inside the rate. There is no quote you can hold for the length of a confirmation call.
Markets your bank won't touch
Paying suppliers in Brazil, India, or South Korea? Most banks refuse or charge punishing spreads on emerging-market currencies.
Slow execution on big tickets
A big trade gets parked at the bank desk. By the time it confirms, the rate you saw is already gone.
Hidden SWIFT and correspondent fees
Correspondent bank charges, lifting fees, beneficiary deductions. The actual cost of a payment is rarely the one you signed off on.
Manual treasury work
Every trade re-keyed from the bank statement into your accounting system. Every month-end becomes a forensic exercise.

FAQ

Questions manufacturers ask us first.

How is pricing structured for institutional volumes?
We charge a transparent markup on top of the interbank rate, disclosed before every trade. For major currency pairs, margins range from 0.15% to 0.75% — based on an example volume above EUR 1 million. Custom pricing is available for sustained high-volume flow.
Can I pay suppliers in emerging-market currencies like BRL, INR, or KRW?
Yes. All three are supported, along with 40+ other currencies across emerging markets in Asia, Africa, the Middle East, and Latin America. Settlement happens through local payment networks where available, which is why we reach corridors most banks refuse.
How fast can you execute a large-ticket trade?
Large-ticket trades are quoted directly by a senior dealer and executed with full quote-locked pricing. Execution happens in minutes, not the next-day queue your bank's desk would put you in. Talk to our specialist about typical ticket sizes and corridor mix.
Can I integrate HansePay with my systems?
API access is available for institutional customers. Standard format exports work with most accounting workflows. Talk to our specialist about your specific integration needs.
Can I lock the rate at execution?
Yes. The exchange rate is locked at the moment of trade execution. The rate shown in the quote is the rate that executes. No slippage between quote and settlement, and no future-dated locking.

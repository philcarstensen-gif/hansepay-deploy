FAQ

Common questions about security.

What happens to my money if HansePay fails?

Customer funds sit in segregated accounts at tier-1 European banks, ring-fenced from HansePay's operating capital. In an insolvency, those funds are not part of the insolvency estate. They are legally protected under MiCAR Article 75 and returned to clients at par value.

Is my data stored inside the EU?

Yes. All customer data is stored and processed inside the European Union, in data centres operated by European or EU-compliant providers. Data handling is GDPR-compliant end to end.

How does HansePay authenticate users?

Strong two-factor authentication is required for login and for every critical action: transactions, FX trades, beneficiary creation, and user management. Authentication methods include time-based one-time passcodes via standard authenticator apps.

Who regulates HansePay?

HansePay operates as a MiCAR-authorised platform under European Union law, supervised by BaFin (Bundesanstalt für Finanzdienstleistungsaufsicht) in Germany. Full licensing details are on our Licenses page.

What certifications does HansePay hold?

ISO 27001 for information security management. GDPR compliance for data handling under EU regulation 2016/679. Independent audits on a recurring cadence.

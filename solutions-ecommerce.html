The hidden cost

Where online retailers
lose margin to FX.

Online retail runs on tight margins. Cross-border FX is one of the biggest invisible cost lines, and one of the easiest to fix.

Spreads on every supplier payment
1.5% to 2.5% extra on every USD, CNY, or BRL transfer to your supplier. Multiplied across hundreds of orders a year, this is your real bank fee.
PayPal & Stripe FX cuts
Up to 4% conversion fee on every cross-border sale. The buyer pays, but the margin disappears before it reaches you.
Markets your bank doesn't reach
Sourcing from Vietnam or Mexico? Selling into Brazil? Most German banks either refuse exotic-currency payments or charge spreads that erase your margin.
No way to fix the rate at quote time
Order Q4 inventory in May, watch the rate move 5% by October. Without execution-time rate locking, your peak-season margin is at the mercy of the spot market.
Wire delays at peak season
Suppliers hold your goods until payment lands. Bank wires take 2 to 3 days. By the time the order ships, you have already lost the launch window.
Reconciliation chaos
Supplier invoices in five currencies, payments routed across multiple banks. Your bookkeeper bills extra, every month.

FAQ

Questions online retailers
ask us first.

Can I pay suppliers in their local currency?
Yes. Sending base currency is EUR. We pay out into the local currency (or USD where commonly used locally) across 45+ markets worldwide, including most emerging markets.
Do you support payments in BRL, MXN, INR, VND, or other emerging-market currencies?
Yes. All four of those currencies are supported, along with 40+ others across emerging markets in Asia, Africa, the Middle East, and Latin America. Settlement happens through local payment networks, not SWIFT correspondent banking, which is why we can reach corridors most banks refuse.
How do I pay Chinese suppliers in CNY without massive wire fees?
We settle CNY payments via local payment rails, not SWIFT. There is no lifting fee, no correspondent bank cut, and no surprise margin on the receiving end. The amount your supplier sees matches the amount in the quote.
What's the minimum volume to work with HansePay?
No fixed minimum. HansePay works with companies trading anywhere from a few thousand euros a year to several hundred million. The only thing that changes is which products make sense for your flow.
Can I integrate HansePay with my systems?
API access is available for institutional customers. Standard format exports work with most accounting workflows. Talk to our specialist about your specific integration needs.
Can I lock the rate at execution?
Yes. The exchange rate is locked at the moment of trade execution. The rate shown in the quote is the rate that executes. No slippage between quote and settlement, and no future-dated locking.
How does this work with my accountant?
Transactions export in standard formats your accounting team already uses. Read-only access for your bookkeeper or accountant is available on request through your account administrator. Talk to our specialist about specific audit-trail requirements.

What we check.

Every IBAN starts with a two-letter country code, followed by two check digits and the country-specific account identifier. We confirm that the country is recognised, that the length matches the expected length for that country (Germany is 22 characters, France is 27, the United Kingdom is 22, and so on), and that the built-in checksum holds.

The checksum is a piece of arithmetic baked into the IBAN standard: by rearranging the IBAN and running it through a fixed mathematical check (mod-97), a single typo will reliably break the result. For German IBANs, we also extract the eight-digit Bankleitzahl from the middle of the number and look it up against the active Deutsche Bundesbank BLZ register, surfacing the bank name, city, and BIC where available.

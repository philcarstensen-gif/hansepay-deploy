The problem

Adding FX is harder than it looks.

Most software platforms eventually hit the same wall. A customer asks for cross-border payment support, or settlement in a different country, or a way to pay suppliers abroad. The product roadmap suddenly includes payments.

The path from there is unappealing. Building it in-house means licensing, banking partnerships, compliance hires, and a multi-year roadmap. Plugging in a generic payments API means thin margins, no FX control, and a brand that isn't yours.

HansePay is the third option. A regulated EU infrastructure layer that platforms embed under their own brand, with the FX, settlement, and compliance handled underneath.

FAQ

What partners ask first.

How long does integration usually take?

Most partners are in sandbox within a week. Production integration timelines depend on scope, but a typical first capability (cross-border payments plus FX) goes live in 4 to 8 weeks. We assign a dedicated technical contact for the duration.

Do we need to be regulated to embed HansePay?

No. You operate as a partner under our regulatory perimeter. Your customers receive a regulated service through Atrya Technologies SIA, authorised under MiCAR. Your compliance footprint stays narrow.

What does the commercial model look like?

We work on revenue share or fixed fee depending on volume and use case. There is no platform fee for the integration itself. Commercial terms are agreed individually so they fit your model.

Can we white-label the experience end-to-end?

Yes. Customers see your brand in your product. HansePay is the regulated provider in the legal terms, but the user experience is yours. Many of our partners' customers don't know we exist, which is the point.

What happens if HansePay has an outage?

Settlement is on-chain via MiCAR-regulated EMTs and runs continuously, including outside banking hours. Our API has standard SLAs. Funds are held in segregated tier-1 bank accounts and protected under EU e-money regulation regardless of operational state.

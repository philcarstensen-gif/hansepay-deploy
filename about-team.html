How we got here

From Hamburg, outward.

HansePay began with a frustration we kept hearing from German business owners. Their bank charged them 2 to 3 percent above mid-market on every cross-border transfer, hid the rate behind the fee, and took three to five days to actually move the money. The financial system was built to work. Just not for them.

In 2023, the founding team began building HansePay in Hamburg. The goal: a cross-border payment platform built for German businesses, regulated end-to-end, designed around the actual rhythms of Mittelstand operations: invoice cycles, supplier payments, treasury reconciliation, audit-readiness. Three years on, the platform settles in seconds across 45+ markets worldwide, including the markets most banks won't touch.

HansePay is the platform German businesses use day-to-day. Built in Hamburg, for the businesses that built Germany's trade economy.

The platform has been constantly cheaper and more reliable than the banking infrastructure it replaces. Lower margins, faster settlement, fewer failed transactions. The numbers compound for businesses that move money across borders every week.
